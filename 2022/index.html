Leader
Communications

The Leader Communications Team empowers church leaders with meaningful, relevant and timely content, resources and information through ResourceUMC.org, The Source e-newsletter and social media channels in multiple languages.

In 2022, approximately 140 original pieces of content were produced in English, Spanish and Korean to help equip church leaders to lead their congregations effectively. Thirty-seven percent of ResourceUMC.org visitors are under the age of 35 and more than 70 percent are under the age of 55.

Highlights include the State of the Church report, Methodists Stand With Ukraine and a collection of new resources to help churches implement the #BeUMC campaign, including videos, graphics, a discussion guide and much more.

A new landing page was created for the Interagency Net-Zero Commitment, a shared space for updates from the agencies on progress toward reaching net zero emissions by 2050. The Leader Communications team worked with the production team to host a two-part webinar with agency attendees of the COP27 climate change conference.

Through a partnership with the General Commission on the Status and Role of Women and the General Commission on Archives and History, we began hosting their websites on ResourceUMC.org and providing other communications support. We also partnered with the National Plan for Hispanic/Latino Ministry and the General Commission on Religion and Race to host the “Somos Metodistas Unidxs Webinar” and worked with Strengthening the Black Church in the 21st Century (Discipleship Ministries) on several projects.

Member
Communications

The Member Communications Team worked with others across the church to provide a diverse array of multimedia content on UMC.org in 2021.

From a series of Tuesdays at the Table conversations by the Connectional Table and a collection of 11 video devotions by our United Methodist bishops to interviews with missionaries in support of United Methodist Global Ministries' Give Love end-of-year giving campaign and a partnership with the General Commission on Archives and History to promote events in England and the U.S. celebrating the 250th anniversary of Francis Asbury's historic journey from Bristol to Philadelphia, collaboration was a key component of developing member content this past year.

Other highlights from the year included stories about church outreach during the COVID pandemic, the 100th episode of the Get Your Spirit in Shape podcast on International Podcast Day and a look back at the September 11 tragedy 20 years later.

Throughout 2021, the Member Communications Team increased production of content in Spanish, French, Korean and Portuguese. Users will note that UMC.org's homepages in every language are now presenting consistent messaging.

Seeker
Communications

Seeker communications includes multi-lingual outreach and evangelism messages through advertising and seeker communication channels.

The Seeker Content Team created 61 original English language articles, 197 Spanish language articles (7 originals and 190 backdated translations), 166 Portuguese backdated translations, 102 French backdated translations, 43 Korean backdated translations, 26 podcast episodes and 65 social media videos.

TikTok provides a promising new avenue for reaching seekers. In the summer of 2021, the agency launched a series of TikTok video ads for the first time. This test proved so successful that the popular spots were reprised for the Advent campaign, and garnered a combined exposure of nearly 2 million for three spots, with 674K, 596K and 603K views.

UM News

UM News focused on 4 major areas of coverage in 2021: The future of the church, the church’s response to the pandemic, efforts to confront and dismantle racism, and uplifting features of United Methodist individuals and congregations transforming the world through their faith.

UM News produced more than 800 original pieces of news content during the year in English, Spanish, Korean, Portuguese and French, and aggregated hundreds of stories from around the church through the UM News Daily Digest. Commentaries on a wide range of topics provided a place for a dialogue of ideas, and a new feature invited people to share personal stories about encounters with racism.

Viewership outside the U.S. grew, most notably in South Korea. A new Friday Digest replaced the former UM News Weekly Digest with a more compelling, visually attractive and easy-to-read weekly e-newsletter, and the frequency of the Korean e-newsletter, DuruAllimi, was changed to weekly.

Coverage highlights in 2021 included multimedia reporting of the plight of immigrants and how United Methodists are responding, an eight-part Ask The UMC series on death and resurrection, and the 250th anniversary of Francis Asbury’s crossing from England to America.

Local Church
Services and Training

Through local church communications support and training, United Methodist Communications helps church leaders polish their communications skills, reach out to their audiences and grow their churches into inviting and inspiring places to worship.

In 2021, the Training Team introduced four new avenues of training to help leaders flourish through communications. They led two facilitated courses (Developing a Strategy for Communication Planning, Communicating During Seasons of Change) and two ecumenical communication conferences (Leveraging Communication, Moving the Church Forward through Communication).

The Local Church Services Team provided marketing coaching, strategy, implementation and resource assistance. Services included comprehensive marketing audits, social media strategy and ad placements, website design, site building and search engine optimization, logo and brand development, design of print materials and customized promotional materials.

Global
Communications

United Methodist Communications is working to implement communications technologies and build infrastructure to help the church reach audiences around the world with the gospel of Jesus Christ.

We built relational bridges, worked with leaders to foster accountability and transparency for successful UMCom- sponsored projects in East Africa, Angola, Nigeria, Zimbabwe, Mozambique, DRC and the Philippines and identified equipment and training needs for South Africa, Burundi and South Congo for radio and television evangelization.

Using the UMConnect text messaging platform, the Global Communications Technology Team reached United Methodists in African countries with important church news updates, lifesaving health messaging, vaccine information and more.

2021 saw the expansion of Voice of Hope radio with a second station site established with financial assistance of $12,000 from United Methodist Communications. UMCom also provided $42,000 to help relaunch Radio Lokole in Kinshasha in the Central Congo Episcopal Area. Grace Radio received initial grant funding of $100,000 to help build a facility and provide equipment through a partnership with The United Methodist Church in Nigeria and the United Methodist Radio Network.

Marketing and Advertising

From Times Square to TikTok, from celebrating our United Methodist core values to connecting with joy, marketing efforts helped raise awareness of what the UMC offers with audiences inside and outside the church.

A new survey reveals that 51% of “spiritual seekers” would be willing to visit a United Methodist church online, and 44% would be willing to visit in person.

The different teams of the Marketing area such as Promotion & Advertising, Public Information, Creative Strategy and Customer Experience worked together to create awareness of The United Methodist Church and its mission and ministries, as well as resources offered by United Methodist Communications to help church leaders communicate more effectively.

A new Neighborly Community national Advent advertising campaign took a lighthearted approach to help individuals struggling with isolation and loneliness find meaningful connections with others through The United Methodist Church. Our disaster relief and national advertising campaigns garnered nearly 459 million impressions.

The #BeUMC campaign invited church members to recognize and embrace the core values that connect the people of The United Methodist Church, live into our mission and remain with The United Methodist Church even in difficult times. Ready-to-use graphics and other resources made it easy for churches and individuals to participate.

Technology and
Customer Experience

United Methodist Communications uses leading-edge technology to create systems that provide for effective data management and ensure that inspirational and informative content reaches the people that need it, while equipping ministry partners to do the same.

My Portal offers an easy way for users to choose the most relevant information they get from United Methodist Communications to get a better customer experience. Users can update their contact information, job roles, interests, newsletter subscriptions and more.

Mapping issues affecting Find-A-Church and My Portal services were resolved, including manually finding missing physical addresses for nearly 500 churches and giving global visibility to churches often lost on maps.

Introduction of the Crisp online chat tool enables web visitors to make inquiries via UMC.org, ResourceUMC.org, UMnews.org and UMChurches.org.

Improvements in web hosting services improved communication about and the workflow for delivering secured sites. By providing SSL for all clients, we kept their Internet connection secure and safeguarded sensitive data.
